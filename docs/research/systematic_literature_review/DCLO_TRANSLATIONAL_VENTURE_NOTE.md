# Translational Venture Note: DCLO Research And Advisory Studio

## Purpose

The SLR identifies a practical gap: institutions are investing in digital systems, but many lack rigorous methods to assess whether people can convert those systems into valued outcomes. Doughnut Economics sharpens the practical offer: institutions need to know whether digital systems help people reach a basic social foundation while avoiding ecological overshoot, extractive platform dependency, surveillance burden, and wasteful device/data practices. A specialised DCLO practice can translate the PhD's construct into field-facing diagnostics, training, and policy evidence while keeping thesis research academically independent.

## Proposed Offer

1. **DCLO capability audits:** assess digital access, literacy, confidence, safety, trust, and outcome pathways for universities, NGOs, public programmes, and CSR initiatives.
2. **Digital public-service readiness studies:** evaluate whether service designs, portals, helplines, and outreach systems are usable for intended populations.
3. **Digital health-literacy and misinformation-risk workshops:** build non-diagnostic training modules for students, youth, community workers, and caregivers.
4. **DPI/DPG governance evidence packs:** map institutional mandates, policy narratives, interoperability claims, and inclusion gaps.
5. **Safe-and-just outcome dashboards and evidence briefs:** turn survey/interview/administrative data into policy-readable outputs that show both social-foundation gains and sustainability/risk constraints.

## Bootstrapped Team Design

Start with 3-5 young graduates at INR 20,000-30,000 per month. Suggested roles:

- Research associate 1: literature screening, citation verification, evidence tables.
- Research associate 2: survey operations, interview scheduling, field documentation.
- Data associate: data cleaning, codebook maintenance, dashboard preparation.
- Communications associate: workshop material, policy brief drafting, stakeholder follow-up.
- Part-time supervisor/reviewer: quality checks, ethics compliance, and final analytical sign-off.

## First 90 Days

- Month 1: train team on DCLO framework, PRISMA screening, source verification, survey ethics, spreadsheet hygiene, and dashboard basics.
- Month 2: pilot one DCLO audit with a friendly institution or NGO; produce a short diagnostic report and training module.
- Month 3: convert pilot into a repeatable service package with templates, pricing, consent forms, and evidence-quality standards.

## Academic Firewall

The venture must not compromise the PhD. Keep client data, thesis data, and personal research notes separate. Do not sell services to thesis participants. Obtain written consent for any field data. Do not use client material in the thesis unless approved by ethics review and documented in the methodology. Disclose the practice arm to the supervisor/SDC if it becomes active during candidature.

## Why It Strengthens The PhD

The venture pathway shows that DCLO is not merely a theoretical construct. It has practical value as a diagnostic and design framework for digital inclusion, service delivery, education, health information, and DPI/DPG governance. It can create field partnerships and policy relevance while the dissertation remains anchored in PRISMA review, construct measurement, reproducible discourse analysis, and documented governance evidence.
